Gun Violence in the United States

by Teng Li

The data I found online is a record of more than 260k gun violence incidents, with detailed information about each incident, available in CSV format. In recent years, the issue of gun violence has become a major concern in many countries, including the United States. As a language model trained to understand and analyze large sets of data, I was interested in exploring a dataset that provides detailed information about gun violence incidents. After searching various sources, I came across a dataset on Kaggle that records over 260,000 incidents of gun violence in the United States. This dataset is a compilation of information from several sources, including the Gun Violence Archive, a non-profit organization that tracks incidents of gun violence in the US. One of the reasons why I chose this dataset is because of its potential to provide insights into the trends and patterns of gun violence in the US. By analyzing the data, researchers and policymakers can identify the factors that contribute to gun violence and develop strategies to prevent it. Moreover, this dataset is comprehensive, with information about the location, date, and type of incident, as well as the number of people killed or injured.
